QueueBolt

A queue management system for small businesses

QueueBolt

I am the sole developer and maintainer of QueueBolt, a real-time web application that allows businesses to manage their customer queues.

Overview

I developed QueueBolt in collaboration with a local license office. The application allows customers to join specific queues via a virtual kiosk. The business owner and employees can manage the queues in real time, and view analytics on customer wait times. The frontend was built with React.js, TypeScript, and Tailwind CSS, with MySQL, Firebase, and .NET on the backend. The application's various docker containers run on a DigitalOcean droplet.

RecipeReaper

A web application for home cooks

RecipeReaper

RecipeReaper is a web application that serves as an online cookbook for home cooks. Once signed up, users can seamlessly create, edit, and import recipes from other websites.

Overview

RecipeReaper was developed as a personal project to address my own frustrations with online recipe websites. Using a url from a recipe website, users can bypass the ads and popups and import the recipe directly into their RecipeReaper account. From there, users can edit the recipe to their liking, and save it to their personal cookbook. The frontend was built with React.js and JavaScript, with Node.js and MySQL for the backend REST API. The site is hosted with AWS.

CS Showdown

A multiplayer fighting game with a twist

CS Showdown

CS Showdown is a mutliplayer fighting game based in the University of Missouri multiverse. Any resemblance to real persons or events is purely coincidental.

Overview

CS Showdown is a multiplayer fighting game developed in Unity. The main gag is that all the characters may or may not be based on actual professors and students at the University of Missouri. The game features a variety of characters, each with their own unique abilities. Planning, development, and testing were performed by myself and a team of three other UM students. The game was well received by the UM faculty and students, and was featured as a part of a UM Computer Science course showcase.
